The Origin

Why Mesi
Does This
Work

"I wanted to ensure that other women would not have to struggle silently through the same barriers."

For more than two decades, Mesi has worked at the point where policy becomes lived reality. Trained in maternal health and HIV/AIDS across East Africa, she witnessed systems failing women in ways that were treated as inevitable.

When she arrived in Canada, she recognised the same failures — less visible, but no less structural. The institutions were different. The silence was the same.

She was among the first to name economic abuse as a policy issue, bringing it into national conversation and onto parliamentary record — before anyone had given it a name, before any framework existed to hold it.

She works on a simple premise: economic harm is not accidental. It is designed — and it can be redesigned. That belief has taken her from community health work to the floors of Parliament, to the IMF, the UN, and 85+ heads of state.

What Mesi Believes

A short manifesto on systems, power, and change.

01

Economic harm is never accidental. Every system that fails women was designed by someone — and it can be redesigned.

02

Real leadership does not come from pretending to be strong. It comes from being honest about the journey.

03

Vulnerability is not something to hide. It is a source of clarity and connection — and it belongs in policy rooms.

04

No woman should have to name her own oppression alone. That is what policy is for. That is what this work is for.
